Whipped cream

Simple Whipped Cream. Light as air, fluffy as clouds, and always perfectly melt-in-your-mouth luscious! This homemade, all-purpose topping made with just 2 ingredients is an essential kitchen know how. It's the crowing finish to all the best desserts. And here I share some of my favorite ways to use it (see the post above for recipes).

  • Time: 5 min
  • Serves: 6
Ingredients Quantity
Vanilla 1/4 tsp
Heavy cream 1 cup
Powdered sugar 3 tbsp

Step 1:Place a large glass or metal mixing bowl and beater blades of a hand mixer in the freezer for 5 minutes or the fridge for 15 minutes.
Step 2: Pour cream into chilled bowl, add sugar, and vanilla if using.
Step 3: Whip mixture on medium-low speed until thickened slightly, about 1 minute.
Step 4: Increase speed to high and whip until stiff peaks that hold their shape form (very stiff peaks that start to loose that wet sheen work best for piping), about 1 minute.
Step 5: Use right away or store in refrigerator in an airtight container using within a couple of hours for the fluffiest results.

Cornbread

My go-to cornbread recipe! It's fluffy, soft and tender, moist, flavorful and the perfect comforting side dish - especially when served warm and finished with whipped honey butter.

  • Time: 40 min
  • Serves: 6
Ingredients Quantity
Cornmeal 3/2 cups
Buttermilk 3/2 cups
All-purpose flour 3/2 cups
Baking powder 3/2 tsp
Baking soda 3/4 tsp
Salt 3/4 tsp
Large eggs 3

Step 1:In a large mixing bowl, whisk together cornmeal and buttermilk until well blended, allow to rest at room temperature for 30 minutes.
Step 2: Meanwhile preheat oven to 375 degrees. Butter a 13 by 9-inch baking dish, set aside.
Step 3: In a mixing bowl, whisk together flour, baking powder, baking soda and salt, set aside.
Step 4: Add melted butter, applesauce, sugar, honey and eggs to cornmeal mixture and whisk until well combine.
Step 5: Stir in dry ingredients and whisk just until combined (small lumps like pancake batter are fine).
Step 6: Pour mixture into prepared baking dish, spread into an even layer and bake 23 - 30 minutes (some said their's takes longer) until toothpick inserted in center comes out clean.
